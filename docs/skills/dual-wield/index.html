Dual Wield Mastery

Grants the ability to wield two one-handed weapons. Both the chance to hit with each weapon and the attack speed are increased. This skill will replace similar effects from other weapon skills. It will also be easier to use higher level offhand weapons. Increases the chance to land double hits.

  • Attack Value +X
  • Attack Speed +X%
  • Item Level without Penalty X
  • Chance for Double Hit +X%

Item Bonuses Governed By Skill

  • There are no item bonuses that are governed by this skill in the unmodified version of the game.

Dual Wielding

  • Grants the ability to equip two one-handed weapons (Sword Weapons, Hafted Weapons or Magic Staffs) in a single weapon slot.
  • The bonuses of this skill apply only when two weapons are equipped in the currently active weapon slot.
    • It will override the bonuses of Sword Weapons, Hafted Weapons and Magic Staffs.
  • When dual wielding, basic attacks alternate between the weapons as follows:
    • For all two-hit animations: one hit with the right-hand weapon, followed by one hit with the left-hand weapon.
    • For the three-hit animation: two hits with the left-hand weapon, followed by one hit with the right-hand weapon.
    • If you are using magic staffs and have the skill Magic Staffs:
      • When the target is within reach, there will be no change in the behavior. The staffs will be used just like regular melee weapons.
      • When the target is out of reach, the same attack animations will be performed, but only one projectile will ever be fired. The projectile is always fired using the right-hand weapon.
  • When dual wielding, weapon-based combat arts always use the right-hand weapon for every hit. This allows the left-hand weapon to be specialized purely for utility.
  • Weapons can be equipped either by right-clicking on them or dropping them directly into a weapon slot. The first weapon you equip will always go to the right hand. The second weapon - to the left hand. If you try to equip another weapon, then you will always replace the one in the left hand. To change the right-hand weapon, it must either be dragged out from the weapon slot, or both weapons need to be removed and equipped again in the desired order.
  • In the inventory screen, the "Weapons" section misleadingly shows that the damage of both weapons is combined.

Skill bonuses

Attack Value +X

  • Increases the character's base Attack Value by the listed amount.
  • Stacks additively with other sources of Attack Value +X.

Attack Speed +X%

  • Increases the Attack Speed of basic attacks.
  • Stacks additively with other sources of Attack Speed +X%.

Execution Speed +X%

  • Not shown in the skill tooltip, but confirmed via testing.
  • Has the same progression as the Attack Speed +X% bonus.
  • Increases the Execution Speed of combat arts influenced by Tactics Lore.
  • Stacks additively with other sources of Execution Speed +X%, like Casting Speed +X%.

Item Level without Penalty X

  • Higher-level weapons (and shields) impose a penalty to the Attack Speed of basic attacks.
  • They also impose a penalty to the Execution Speed of combat arts influenced by Tactics Lore.
    • This penalty is half as much as the penalty to Attack Speed.
  • The higher the item level is above the character's, the more severe the penalties.
    • The formula is: Multiplier = MIN(1, ((CharacterLevel + 9) / (ItemLevel + 9))2)
  • This bonus increases the character's effective level (expressed as X) by 0.5 per skill point, thereby reducing the penalties.
    • This does not affect the Maximum Usable Item Level; characters still cannot equip items above this limit.
  • In the tooltip, X is rounded down and capped at Maximum Usable Item Level minus 2. Despite what the tooltip shows, X is not actually rounded down or capped.
  • The reduction is applied only when a relevant weapon is equipped in the currently active weapon slot.
  • When multiple weapons are equipped (e.g., Dual Wield) or a weapon and a shield are equipped, only the highest penalty among them is imposed; these penalties do not stack.
  • Penalties from other sources, such as debuffs, stack additively with the penalty from equipped items.

Chance for Double Hit +X%

  • This bonus relates to the chance of performing a special attack animation that involves an extra hit.
    • In the case of dual wield, this means 3 hits instead of 2.
  • This special attack animation concerns only basic attacks and has a chance to occur even without the respective weapon skill.
  • The skill simply increases the chance for it to occur. Skill mastery is not required, but it will raise the chance even further.
  • Do not confuse with the item bonus Chance for Double Hit +X%.
